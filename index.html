Our Gallery

Welcome to our gallery, where the beauty of art meets the resilience of our teenagers. Here, you'll find a collection of exquisite artifacts crafted by the skilled hands of those who've undergone the Opportunity Pathways Initiative. Each piece tells a story of transformation, showcasing the talent and creativity that emerges from second chances. Take a moment to explore and be inspired by the remarkable artworks of our young artisans.

Summer hat. Made from the lightest of the fibers and intricate beadwork to transform it to a piece of art.

Mshipi. This leather belt is brought to life by the beautiful beach-inspired beadwork.

Ka-mkeka. Tribal place mats meticulously handcrafted from leather and beadwork.

Teo. Embellished with beads and customized to be deeper for fruit storage.

Wooded kitchenware crafted by the teenagers.

Beaded Bracelet.

Beaded choker. This is aptly named Urembo choker. Urembo means beauty in Swahili.

Nyeusi choker. Nyeusi is dark-colored in Swahili. This choker is brought to life by the vibrant color combinations.

Kazuri earrings. Kazuri means small and beautiful in Swahili.
